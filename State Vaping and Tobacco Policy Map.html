Methodology & Data Sources

This database provides comprehensive, up-to-date information on state vaping and flavored tobacco policies across all 50 states and the District of Columbia.

Data Categories

  • Flavored E-Cigarette Restrictions: Comprehensive bans, partial restrictions, and exemptions
  • Menthol Cigarette Bans: State-level prohibitions on menthol cigarettes
  • E-Cigarette Excise Taxes: Tax rates, structures (wholesale %, per mL, retail %, per cartridge)
  • Indoor Vaping Restrictions: Smoke-free laws covering e-cigarettes in workplaces, restaurants, and bars
  • Minimum Age Laws: State Tobacco 21 laws (federal T21 applies to all states)
  • Licensing Requirements: Retailer licensing and fee structures
  • Local Restrictions: Notable city and county-level policies

Primary Data Sources

  • Truth Initiative: Flavored Tobacco Restrictions Q2 2025 (truthinitiative.org)
  • Campaign for Tobacco-Free Kids: State Policy Database 2024 (tobaccofreekids.org)
  • Tax Foundation: Vaping Taxes by State 2025 (taxfoundation.org)
  • CDC STATE System: State Tobacco Activities Tracking and Evaluation System (cdc.gov/statesystem)
  • FDA: Tobacco 21 Resources (fda.gov)
  • State Legislative Sources: Direct review of state statutes and regulations

Data Currency

All data is current as of December 2024/January 2025. This database includes the most recent policy changes including:

  • California's expanded flavor ban (Jan 1, 2025)
  • Rhode Island's permanent flavor ban (Jan 1, 2025)
  • Utah's flavor ban upheld by federal court (March 2025)
  • Ohio and Texas flavor bans (2025)
  • North Carolina and Wisconsin FDA-authorized product requirements (July-Sep 2025)

Federal Context

Tobacco 21: Federal law prohibits the sale of tobacco products to anyone under 21 years of age nationwide (effective Dec 20, 2019).

FDA Menthol Rule: The FDA withdrew proposed federal menthol and flavored cigar bans in January 2025. State and local policies continue to vary.

Limitations

  • Local ordinances are noted where significant but not comprehensively cataloged (nearly 400 localities have flavor restrictions)
  • Enforcement practices may vary by jurisdiction
  • Policies are subject to legal challenges and may change
  • Tax rates reflect statutory rates; actual implementation may vary
